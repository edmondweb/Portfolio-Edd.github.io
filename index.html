Real Estate Market Analysis

Objective

To analyze real estate pricing trends in Metropolitan Melbourne and determine whether the average price per square metre (PSM) of residential properties exceeds $6800, using statistical methods and hypothesis testing.

Project Details

Data Source & Preparation

  • The dataset was sourced from web-scraped real estate listings.
  • A new variable, PSM (Price per Square Metre), was computed as Price / Building Area.
  • Missing values in the dataset were cleaned to ensure data integrity.

Preliminary Analysis

  • Mean PSM: $7673.09 → Higher than the $6800 benchmark.
  • Median PSM: $6919.12 → Slightly lower than the mean, indicating right-skewed distribution.
  • Standard Deviation: $4862.60 → High variation in property pricing.
  • Interquartile Range (IQR): $4173.19 → Further confirms variability.

Data Visualization

  • Histogram: Showed a right-skewed distribution with a few high-value properties.
  • Boxplot: Highlighted outliers in the dataset.

Hypothesis Testing

  • Null Hypothesis (H₀): The mean PSM is $6800.
  • Alternative Hypothesis (H₁): The mean PSM is greater than $6800.
  • t-test Result: t-statistic of 12.61 with p-value < 2.2e-16 (highly significant), confirming the mean PSM exceeds $6800.

Business Impact

  • Helped CityScape Insights, a real estate startup, understand pricing trends in Melbourne.
  • Provided data-driven validation for market pricing strategies.

Skills & Technologies Used

R dplyr ggplot2 Statistical Testing

Economic & Nutritional Analysis: Determinants of Food Budget Constraints

Objective

To investigate the economic and structural factors influencing food affordability, using regression modeling and hypothesis testing to assess the impact of GDP, education expenditure, agriculture, exports, and natural resource rents on the percentage of the population with a low food budget.

Project Details

Data Overview & Preparation

  • Dataset included country-level data on GNI per capita, government education expenditure, agricultural value added, exports, and natural resource rents.
  • Selected key independent variables for modeling and handled missing values to ensure accurate regression analysis.

Regression Analysis & Hypothesis Testing

  • Ordinary Least Squares (OLS) Regression: Modeled how economic variables affect the percentage of the population with a low food budget.
  • Log Transformation: Applied log transformation to stabilize variance and improve model accuracy.
  • Multicollinearity Check (VIF): Ensured independent variables were not highly correlated.
  • Heteroskedasticity Test (Breusch-Pagan Test): Checked for non-constant variance in the model.
  • HAC Standard Errors: Used heteroskedasticity-consistent standard errors for robust coefficient estimation.

Impact of Regional Differences

  • Included regional dummy variables to analyze variations across different geographic locations.
  • Conducted ANOVA to compare models with and without regional dummies, assessing whether location significantly impacts food affordability.

Data Visualization & Tools Used

  • ggplot2 for scatter plots showing relationships between variables.
  • Linear regression analysis to quantify the influence of economic factors.
  • ANOVA testing to assess the importance of regional differences.

Key Insights & Business Impact

  • Identified economic indicators that significantly affect food affordability.
  • Demonstrated regional disparities in food budget constraints.
  • Provided policy recommendations for improving food security through economic and educational investments.

Skills & Technologies Used

R Regression Analysis ggplot2 ANOVA
